Do You Need a UPS? Yes If Your Power Is Unstable

Last Updated: May 14, 2026 5 Min Read PC & Electronics

One sudden blackout can corrupt your SSD, kill a $1500 graphics card, or destroy hours of unsaved work. If your electricity flickers or goes out even once a month, you need a UPS (Uninterruptible Power Supply).

An $80 UPS is cheaper than replacing a $400 GPU. The math is simple. This guide explains why.

What Is a UPS and What Does It Do?

A UPS is a large battery that sits between your wall outlet and your PC. When power is normal, it charges itself and passes clean electricity to your devices. When power cuts out, it instantly switches to battery mode in 2-4 milliseconds.

That gives you 5-30 minutes to save your work and shut down safely. It also protects against power surges that can fry components.

Bottom line: Only a UPS protects you from blackouts. A surge protector is useless if the power goes out.

Real Cost of No UPS: Sudden power loss during a Windows Update can corrupt your OS. Power loss during gaming can kill an SSD or GPU. Repair costs: $100 for data recovery, $300+ for new SSD, $400+ for new GPU. An $80 UPS pays for itself after one saved incident.

What Size UPS Do You Need?

UPS size is measured in VA (Volt-Amps) or Watts. For a typical PC setup, use this guide:

  1. 600VA / 360W: Office PC, monitor, router. Gives 8-15 min backup. Cost: $70-90.
  2. 1000VA / 600W: Gaming PC with mid-range GPU like RTX 4060, one monitor. Gives 5-10 min backup. Cost: $120-160.
  3. 1500VA / 900W: High-end gaming PC with RTX 4080/4090, dual monitors. Gives 5-8 min backup. Cost: $200-280.
How to Calculate: Add up the wattage of your PC power supply + monitor + router. Buy a UPS with 20% more capacity. Example: 650W PC + 30W monitor = 680W. Get a 900W UPS minimum. Don't max out the UPS capacity.

What Should You Plug Into a UPS?

Plug in: PC, monitor, Wi-Fi router, external hard drives.

Do NOT plug in: Laser printers, space heaters, coffee makers. They draw too much power and will trip the UPS instantly. They also don't need backup power.

How Long Does a UPS Battery Last?

The battery lasts 3-5 years. You'll know it needs replacement when backup time drops under 2 minutes or the UPS beeps constantly. Replacement batteries cost $25-60 and are easy to swap.

My Personal Rule

If your PC + monitor cost more than $500 total, buy a UPS. If you work from home or have important data, buy a UPS. If your lights flicker during storms, buy a UPS.

I lost a 1TB SSD with all my family photos to a power surge in 2023. $200 in data recovery fees. I bought an $85 UPS the next day. Haven't had a problem since.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can a UPS power my PC for hours?

No. It's for safe shutdown, not extended use. 5-15 minutes is standard. If you need hours, you need a generator, which costs $500+.

Does a UPS use a lot of electricity?

No. It uses 10-20W to keep the battery charged. That's about $2-4 per year in electricity cost.

Can I use a UPS for my TV and console?

Yes. A 600VA UPS is perfect for a 55" TV + PS5/Xbox. Gives you time to save your game and turn off safely during a blackout.

What's the best brand?

APC, CyberPower, and Eaton are the most reliable. Avoid no-name brands. The battery quality matters. Buy from Amazon or a local computer store with warranty.
