How to Check Internet Speed: Ping Google

Ping test shows how fast your computer talks to Google's server. Lower = better for gaming, Zoom, Netflix.

Step-by-Step Windows:

  1. Press Windows + R on your keyboard. A "Run" box appears.
  2. Type cmd and press Enter. Black window called "Command Prompt" opens.
  3. Type ping google.com -t and press Enter. Don't close the window.
  4. Look at "time=XXms" on each line. This is your ping.
  5. Press Ctrl + C to stop the test.

How to Read Results:

0-50ms: Excellent. Perfect for gaming & video calls.
50-100ms: Good. Normal browsing is fine.
100-150ms: Okay. Might lag in games.
150ms+: Slow. Call your internet provider.

Mac Users:

Open "Terminal" → type ping google.com → Enter. Same results.

Is Your Laptop Overheating? Check Temperature

Laptops slow down or shut off when too hot. Check your CPU/GPU temp in 30 seconds.

Method 1: Task Manager - Quick Check

  1. Press Ctrl + Shift + Esc to open Task Manager.
  2. Click "Performance" tab on the left.
  3. Click "CPU" or "GPU". Look for temperature. If not shown, use Method 2.

Method 2: HWInfo - Exact Temperature

  1. Google "HWInfo download" → Download free version from hwinfo.com
  2. Install → Run HWInfo → Click "Sensors-only" → Run.
  3. Scroll to "CPU" section. Look for "CPU Package" temp.
  4. Scroll to "GPU" section for graphics card temp.

Safe Temperatures:

Under 60°C: Cool. Perfect.
60-80°C: Warm. Normal under load/gaming.
80-90°C: Hot. Clean fans, use cooling pad.
90°C+: Danger. Shut down, check for dust.

Find What’s Eating Your Storage in 1 Minute

Hard drive full? Find the biggest folders and delete junk fast.

Windows 11/10 Steps:

  1. Click Start → Settings ⚙️
  2. Click "System" → "Storage"
  3. Wait 10 seconds. Windows scans your drive.
  4. Click each category: "Apps", "Documents", "Pictures", "Temporary files"
  5. Click "Temporary files" → Check all boxes → "Remove files". This alone frees 5-20GB.
  6. Go back → Click "Other" to see huge folders. Delete what you don't need.

Pro Tip: TreeSize Free

Download "TreeSize Free" from jam-software.com. It shows a map of your entire hard drive. Biggest folders = biggest boxes. Delete old downloads, videos, games you don't play.

Mac Users:

Apple Menu → About This Mac → Storage → Manage → See recommendations.
