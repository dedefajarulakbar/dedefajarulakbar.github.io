Is Your Laptop Overheating? Check Temperature Now

Laptops slow down or shut off when too hot. Dust, old thermal paste, or blocking vents are common causes. Check your CPU/GPU temp in 30 seconds.

Method 1: Task Manager - Quick Check

  1. Press Ctrl + Shift + Esc to open Task Manager.
  2. Click "Performance" tab on the left sidebar.
  3. Click "CPU" or "GPU". Look for temperature at the bottom. If not shown, use Method 2.

Method 2: HWInfo - Exact Temperature

  1. Google "HWInfo download" → Download free version from hwinfo.com
  2. Install → Run HWInfo → Select "Sensors-only" → Click Run.
  3. Scroll to "CPU [#0]" section. Look for "CPU Package" or "Core Max" temp.
  4. Scroll to "GPU [#0]" section for your graphics card temp.
  5. Leave it running while gaming/working to see max temps.

Safe Temperatures Guide:

Under 60°C: Cool. Perfect. Idle temps.
60-80°C: Warm. Normal when gaming, video editing, or heavy browsing.
80-90°C: Hot. Clean your laptop fans, use a cooling pad, close unused apps.
90°C+: Danger Zone. Your laptop may throttle or shut down. Clean dust ASAP, repaste CPU if old.

Pro Tip: Put your laptop on a hard flat surface. Beds and blankets block vents and make temps jump 20°C+.

Mac Users:

Download "Macs Fan Control" or "iStat Menus". Apple hides temps by default. Same safe ranges apply.
